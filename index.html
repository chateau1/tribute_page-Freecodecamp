Fred Sasakamoose

One of the first Canadian Indigenous players in the NHL

Life & Career

  • As a child he was taught to skate by his grandfather, who was deaf and mute.
  • He was forced to attend a residential school 96km away from where his family lived, along with his brother. He was there for 9 years.
  • During this time he experienced emotion, physical and sexual abuse. He only experienced happiness while skating and playing hockey.
  • Fred started playing junior hockey with the Moose Jaw Canucks
  • When he was 20 years old he was called up to play for the Chicago Blackhawks.
  • He was noted for his speed, dancing footwork and hard slapshot.
